i would like to join the stimulating debate about the all time west indian eleven with my team and related comments

12th man - everton weeks

rationale
gordon grenidge - best opening bat in the modern era.. he however barely makes it over roy fredricks (who did well in austriala but fredricks was a bit one dimensional)
conrad hunte - greatest opener of his era in the wi - carried the batting without partners
george headly - second best batsman of his generation 50 per cent chance he will make a century and averages 60 with the bat. best batsman in the side
viv richards - most devastating batsman of any era... will deplete the opposition bowling
frank worrell - almost 50 average (40 plus) - best captaincy skills of any west indian
gary sobers - all rounder who will make runs and give depth to the bowling attack with either medium pace or left arm spin (complements gibbs)
derek walcott - wicket keeper with a fifty average
lance gibbs best offspinner of his generation - takes wickets while containing the runs of the opposition
micheal holding - speed merchant
malcolm marshall - moves ball at pace (90mph)
curtley ambrose - bounce and uncertainty of the off stump line
weeks - 12th man - consistency of performance - especially against australia

why lara does not make it. again (for all you emoticons out there - why lara DOES NOT MAKE IT)
FIRST - lara batted in a time of helmets (cricket was safer)
SECOND - lara batted in a time when bowlers had only one bouncer per over
THIRD - lara batted in a time when the only major bowlers around were glen mcgrath and shane warne.
FOURTH - he was not a consistent match winner - just exciting to watch. you could depend on viv. you could depend on headly..these guys were big second innings players. end of story
