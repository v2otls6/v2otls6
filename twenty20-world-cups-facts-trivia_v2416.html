Twenty20 World Cups - Facts & Trivia

  1. Ab de Villiers (South Africa) and Younis Khan (Pakistan) took the most number of catches in the series (6 catches each).
  2. Adam Gilchrist had the most number of dismissals by a wicket-keeper in 2007 T20 World Cup – 9 dismissals
  3. Australia lost to Zimbabwe in their opening match in the 2007 edition of T20 World Cup
  4. Chris Gayle hit 10 sixes – the maximum in a T20 world cup innings – against South Africa
  5. Chris Gayle, West Indies (117 runs) is the only person to score a century in a T20 World Cup match. This was against South Africa
  6. Craig McMillan of New Zealand scored the maximum number of sixes (13 sixes) in 2007 T20 cup
  7. Gayle and Smith scored 145 runs in partnership for the 1st wicket against South Africa – the highest partnership for any wicket
  8. Gayle has scored the highest number of runs from fours and sixes in an innings – 88 runs (7 fours and 10 sixes) against South Africa
  9. Highest successful run chase in the world cup T20 was by South Africa against West Indies – chased the target of 205 runs
  10. India’s highest score in a T20 world cup match is against England – 218/4
  11. Kenyan team was all out for 73 runs against New Zealand – Lowest total in a T20 world cup match
  12. Mathew Hayden of Australia is the highest run-getter in T20 world cup series (265 runs)
  13. Only one match was abandoned in the 2007 World Cup – the group match between India and Scotland in Durban
  14. Sanath Jayasuriya of Sri Lanka conceded 64 runs in 4 overs against Pakistan at Johannesburg – the most number of runs conceded in an innings
  15. Sri Lanka has a dubious record of winning by the biggest margin in terms of runs and losing by the biggest margin in terms of wickets – won against Kenya by 172 runs and lost to Australia by 10 wickets
  16. The first hat-trick of World Cup was taken by Brett lee against Bangladesh
  17. The highest team total in world cup T20 is 260/6 by Sri Lanka against Kenya
  18. Umar Gul of Pakistan took the most number of wickets (13 wickets at an avg of 11.92) in the series
  19. West Indies conceded the most number of extra runs (28 extras) in their T20 World Cup 2007 match against South Africa at Johannesburg
  20. Yuvraj Singh became the first player in T20 to hit six 6s in an over. This was against England in T20 world cup 2007 and the unfortunate bowler was Stuart Broad
  21. Yuvraj Singh has scored the fastest fifty (in 12 balls) in T20 world cup. This was against England
  22. Yuvraj Singh was a close second with 12 sixes
