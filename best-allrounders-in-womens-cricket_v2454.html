This is my first post on this forum! I am a huge fan of women's cricket and I love watching, keeping nerdy records/scores and some playing too. I specially love allrounders. They are the ones who can change the game with their skills and versatility. In this post which I have compiled from ranknigs performances and potential, I want to share my opinion on the 10 best women's cricket all-rounders in the world, taken from all the top teams. 

10. Nida Dar (Pakistan)

Nida Dar is the highest-ranked all-rounder from Pakistan and one of the best in the world. She is a powerful batter and a clever spinner who can score quick runs and take crucial wickets. She has played many T20Is for Pakistan and has been their leading run-scorer and wicket-taker. She is the only Pakistani woman to score a T20I century, which she did against Sri Lanka in 2019.

9. Kathryn Bryce (Scotland)

Kathryn Bryce is the captain and star player of Scotland, who has made a name for herself in the international arena. She is a versatile batter and a consistent bowler who can bat at any position and bowl in any situation. She is one of the top all-rounders in the world according to the ICC T20I rankings. She has played several T20Is for Scotland and has been their most reliable performer with both bat and ball. She was named as one of the five breakout stars of women's cricket in 2020 by ESPNcricinfo.

8. Chamari Athapaththu (Sri Lanka)

Chamari Athapaththu is the captain and leading batter of Sri Lanka, who has been consistently performing at the highest level for her team. She is a stylish batter and a handy spinner who can open the innings and bowl in the middle overs. She is among the best all-rounders in the world according to the ICC T20I rankings. She has played many T20Is for Sri Lanka and has been their top run-scorer and a key wicket-taker. She holds the record for the highest individual score by a Sri Lankan woman in T20Is against Australia in 2019.

7. Natalie Sciver-Brunt (England)

Natalie Sciver-Brunt is one of the most dynamic and explosive all-rounders in the world, who can bat anywhere in the order and bowl with pace and variation. She is a destructive batter and a lethal bowler who can hit big shots and take key wickets. She is among the best all-rounders in the world according to the ICC T20I rankings. She has played many T20Is for England and has been their second-highest run-scorer and third-highest wicket-taker. She was named as one of the five cricketers of the year by Wisden in 2020.

6. Sophie Devine (New Zealand)

Sophie Devine is the captain and talisman of New Zealand, who has been dominating with both bat and ball for over a decade. She is an aggressive batter and a fast bowler who can open the batting and bowling with equal flair. She is one of the top all-rounders in the world according to the ICC T20I rankings. She has played many T20Is for New Zealand and has been their highest run-scorer and wicket-taker. She holds the record for the fastest fifty by a woman in T20Is, which she scored off just 18 balls against Sri Lanka in 2020.

5. Deepti Sharma (India)

Deepti Sharma is the backbone of India's bowling and lower-order batting, who has been delivering consistent performances for her team. She is a solid batter and a skillful spinner who can bat in the middle order and bowl in the powerplay and the death overs. She is one of the top all-rounders in the world according to the ICC T20I rankings. She has played many T20Is for India and has been their most economical bowler and a valuable run-scorer. She is the youngest Indian woman to score an ODI century, which she did against Ireland in 2017 at the age of 19.

4. Amelia Kerr (New Zealand)

Amelia Kerr is the prodigy of New Zealand cricket, who has been breaking records and making headlines since her debut. She is a talented batter and a cunning leg-spinner who can bat in the top order and bowl with accuracy and guile. She is one of the best all-rounders in the world according to the ICC T20I rankings. She has played several T20Is for New Zealand and has been their most effective bowler and a consistent run-scorer. She holds the record for the highest individual score by a woman in ODIs, which is 232 not out against Ireland in 2018, when she also took 5 for 17 in the same match.

3. Hayley Matthews (West Indies)

Hayley Matthews is the star all-rounder of West Indies, who has been instrumental in their success in recent years. She is a fluent batter and a sharp spinner who can open the batting and bowling with aggression and skill. She is the second-best all-rounder in the world according to the ICC T20I rankings. She has played many T20Is for West Indies and has been their second-highest run-scorer and wicket-taker. She was the player of the match in the final of the ICC Women's World T20 in 2016, when she scored 66 off 45 balls and took 1 for 13 against Australia.

2. Ashleigh Gardner (Australia)

Ashleigh Gardner is the premier all-rounder of Australia, who has been dominating with both bat and ball for the world champions. She is a powerful batter and a spinning all-rounder who can bat in the middle order and bowl in any phase of the game. She is the best all-rounder in the world according to both the ICC ODI and T20I rankings. She has played many ODIs and T20Is for Australia and has been their third-highest run-scorer and wicket-taker in both formats. She was named as one of the five cricketers of the year by Wisden in 2021.

1. Ellyse Perry (Australia)

Ellyse Perry is the undisputed queen of women's cricket, who has been ruling with both bat and ball for over a decade. She is a classy batter and a pacey bowler who can bat at any position and bowl with pace and swing. She is widely regarded as one of the greatest all-rounders of all time, across genders and formats. She has played many ODIs and T20Is for Australia and has been their highest run-scorer and wicket-taker in both formats. She is the only player, male or female, to score 1000 runs and take 100 wickets in T20Is. She has won the ICC Women's Cricketer of the Year award twice, in 2017 and 2019.
